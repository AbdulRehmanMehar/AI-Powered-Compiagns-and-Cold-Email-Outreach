## Persona 1: `pe_portfolio_tech_leaders`
### Private Equity Portfolio Companies — Forced Tech Consolidation

**Who They Are:**
- Title: CTO, VP of Engineering, Director of IT, Head of Technology
- Company size: 200-2000 employees (portfolio companies post-acquisition)
- Industry: Private equity portfolio companies across sectors — often manufacturing, healthcare, financial services, business services
- Situation: A PE firm acquired their company (or merged 2-3 companies) and now demands tech stack consolidation, cost reduction, and "digital transformation" within 12-18 months

**Why They'd Buy ($100-500k):**
PE firms set aggressive timelines for value creation. Post-acquisition, the tech leader inherits 2-5 redundant systems that need to be consolidated into one platform. Internal teams are already stretched maintaining existing systems. The PE operating partner is demanding results by next quarter. This isn't optional — it's a mandate from the board/PE firm with a hard deadline. They have a "technology transformation budget" carved out specifically for this.

**The Crap They Deal With:**
- Consulting firms (McKinsey, Deloitte) charge $3M for a PowerPoint roadmap and then leave
- Big integrators (Accenture, Infosys) take 18 months and 40-person teams for what should be a 3-month project
- Offshore teams write unmaintainable code that creates MORE technical debt
- Internal teams are demoralized from acquisition chaos — best engineers are leaving
- Every vendor promises "seamless integration" but nobody understands their specific legacy mess
- PE operating partners who have zero technical understanding but demand weekly progress reports
- Previous CTO left, so institutional knowledge walked out the door

**The Hunger:**
A small, senior engineering team that can assess their multi-system mess, build a consolidation plan, and start executing in weeks — not months. Someone who's done legacy-to-modern migrations and understands that "rip and replace" is a fantasy. They need a partner who can show visible progress to PE partners every 2 weeks.

**Spending Logic:**
The PE firm allocated $5-20M for "operational improvements" post-acquisition. A $200-400k engagement for tech consolidation is a rounding error. The ROI is clear: reduce overlapping SaaS costs ($500k+/year), reduce headcount on redundant systems, and increase platform velocity for future bolt-on acquisitions. If they DON'T consolidate, the PE firm's value creation thesis fails.

**Fears:**
- Migration that takes down production systems during peak season
- Best engineers quitting during the transition (they've already lost 3)
- PE partners losing confidence and bringing in their own "technology advisor" who overrides decisions
- Spending $2M on a failed consolidation that resets the timeline
- Being replaced as CTO because progress is too slow
